Living in Taiwan, we should learn more about the weather and the climate

The weather in Taiwan is not always predictable. For instance, we have typhoons, weather fronts, and tornadoes. As a citizen in Taiwan, we should learn as much as we can about the weather so we will be caught off guard.

First, I'd like to introduce "typhoons". A typhoon is an intense area of low atmospheric pressure and it contains a lot of humidity. Typhoons are commonly developed in tropical areas. A typhoon can cause several serious problems like floodings or mudslides, but it can also bring a lot of rain to the area.

Next, I'd like to talk about weather fronts. There are basically four kinds of fronts in the world. But in Taiwan, normally, we only encounter three kinds of fronts, cold front, warm front, and stationary front. How they form are literally the same. They form when two air masses (different temperature) meet. When the cold air mass is stronger than the warm air mass, we get a cold front and vice versa. A stationary front forms when the cold air mass and the warm air mass have the same power and it usually brings a lot of water.

Finally, let's talk about tornado. This kind of weather does not occur in Taiwan often, because there are many tall buildings to stop them from growing.
